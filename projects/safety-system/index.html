Safety System

Problem

Managing, maintaining and reporting on safety records for all locations in the corporation to align with 5S[1] Lean Manufacturing best practices to build a safety-first culture and world-class manufacturing facilities.

Discovery

  • Identified key areas for safety record management, including incident reporting, employee training, and onboarding procedures.
  • Recognized the need for comprehensive site inspection tools to assess safety compliance.
  • Explored strategies to integrate safety protocols with existing company processes and systems.

Solutions

Company Safety Training System

  • Developed customized safety modules compliant with WHMIS standards.
  • Integrated training modules into the corporate intranet for easy access and tracking of employee progress.

Site Inspection Form

  • Implemented a digital inspection form allowing real-time assessment with photo capture and annotation features.
  • Enhanced visibility of carry-over items from previous inspections to prioritize corrective actions.
  • Incorporated 5S score integration to align safety efforts with Lean Manufacturing principles.

5S Survey

  • Expanded 5S methodology to include safety as a core component.
  • Conducted regular surveys to evaluate safety practices and maintain facility standards.
  • Streamlined reporting and data visualization for executive review and decision-making.

Safety Committee Meeting Minutes

  • Automated recording and tracking of safety committee meeting minutes.
  • Integrated attendance tracking to monitor employee participation and compliance.

Toolbox Meeting Minutes

  • Digitized toolbox meeting minutes for efficient documentation and sharing.
  • Facilitated communication of near-miss reports and safety updates between management and shop floor employees.

Results

  • Achieved significant improvements in facility safety ratings and environmental review assessments.
  • Received commendations from external auditors for comprehensive record-keeping and safety management practices.
  • Enhanced technician readiness with streamlined training documentation for client site visits.
  • Fostered a proactive safety culture with transparent communication and swift issue resolution.

Technology Used

  • Utilized EyeFi Wifi SD Cards for rapid image transfer during site inspections.
  • Deployed Fujitsu Lifebook tablets for efficient data collection and reporting before the advent of iPads.
  • Leveraged Adobe Captivate and custom Microsoft SQL databases for Learning Management System integration.

  1. 5S methodology emphasizes organization and efficiency in the workplace:

    1. Sort (Seiri): Eliminate unnecessary items.
    2. Straighten (Seiton): Organize remaining items for easy access.
    3. Shine (Seisou): Keep the workspace clean and well-maintained.
    4. Standardize (Seiketsu): Establish and enforce standardized procedures.
    5. Sustain (Shitsuke): Maintain and improve the 5S practices consistently.
